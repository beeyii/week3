I've traveled through Three out of the Seven Seas.

If I were to tell you about my whole Navy experience I think this page would turn into a novel, so let me tell you briefly about my Navy experience.
On August 27, 2013 I began bootcamp in Great Lakes, Illinois. In bootcamp I was given the leadership position in my division. I was the administrative leader and made sure everyone's file and paperwork were ready to go when the executive leaders asked about each individual's report. Eight weeks later, I completed my basic training and I was sent to Meridian, Mississippi to complete my Logistics Specialists School. While at the Logistics Specialists School studied supply chain management, accounting, finance, and operations. The software I used in the Navy was called Logistics Command Management Information System (NALCOMIS). Through NALCOMIS we purchased materials, parts, supplies, etc. we built requisitions; performed ad-hoc analysis; pooled data; planned servicing and organized maintenance schedule.
On February 19, 2014 I landed in Japan to perform my duties as a Logistics Specialist. I was extremely nervous and did not know what to expect and what was going to be expected of me. I was as nervous as anyone on their first job after college. Even though I learned how to be a Logistics Specialist and how to procure items and how to perform ad-hoc queries, but what if I don't have the right answer?
